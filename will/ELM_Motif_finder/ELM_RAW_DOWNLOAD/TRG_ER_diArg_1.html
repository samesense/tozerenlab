 Abstract

Proteins involved in the various steps and compartments along the secretory pathway have to be sorted and selected to be kept within the pathway while the secretory cargo progresses. The cytoplasmic side of the integral membrane proteins is equiped with various signals allowing them to remain in their physiological location and or to be retrieved from downstream compartments.
Located at the N-terminus of type II membrane proteins (cytoplasmic in this topology) the di Arginine signal confers retention of ER resident proteins and retrieving from the post ER compartments via a retrograde transport pathway.
Occasionally this motif is found at the C-terminus of type I membrane proteins or even in a loop of multi membrane spanning proteins.
